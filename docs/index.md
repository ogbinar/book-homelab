# Bahay Bahayan

## Build Your First Homelab

> **Start Small. Learn Fast. Build What's Yours.**

Kumusta! This is a Filipino-first guide to building your own homelab — your personal server at home where you run your own services, control your own data, and learn real infrastructure skills. Start small, learn fast, and build something you can actually keep running.

Whether you're a student, a career shifter, a hobbyist, or just curious — this book is for you.

The book grows like a school ladder: `Prep` to `Postdoc`. But the real point is simpler than that. We keep everything rooted in the house, so the ideas stay easy to remember: power, water, locks, front desk, utility cabinet, game room, and study room.

---

## Why This Book?

Maraming reasons kung bakit natin ito ginawa:

| Value | What It Means |
|-------|---------------|
| **Learning by Doing** | Every chapter produces something you can touch and use |
| **Open Source First** | We use free, open-source tools — no vendor lock-in |
| **Privacy as a Human Right** | Your data, your rules. No more "trust us" with SaaS |
| **Self-Hosting as Ownership** | Building your own infrastructure means keeping control of your tools and data |
| **No Gatekeeping** | You don't need a CS degree. You need curiosity and 30 minutes a week |
| **Household First** | Every service should map to a real home need, not just a cool demo |

The journey also follows a maturity ladder:

|---------|-------|------|

| 5 | Keeping It Alive | 1 hr |

### [Part II: Lumaki (Growing Your Lab)](part-ii.md)

Build a multi-service homelab that you actually use daily. Learn the patterns that scale.

| Chapter | Topic | Time |
|---------|-------|------|
| 7 | The Reverse Proxy + Remote Access | 3.5 hrs |
| 8 | Multiple Services, One System | 3 hrs |
| 9 | Your Data, Safe | 2 hrs |

| 11 | Monitoring: Eyes on Everything | 2 hrs |

Some topics live outside the core path and are intentionally left for future expansion: game hosting, local hosted LLMs, and Dokploy-style service operations. They belong later because they make more sense once the reader already has the basic house in order.

## About the Author

**Myk Ogbinar** is a full-time corporate professional and part-time PhD student with a long-running interest in data, AI, and community building. His work tends to favor useful systems, clear thinking, and honest execution.

Outside of work and study, he keeps up with learning, books, games, and family life. That mix of responsibilities is why this book stays practical, budget-aware, and focused on what you can actually maintain.

This guide is heavily inspired by the spirit of **[Data Engineering Pilipinas (DEP)](https://dataengineering.ph/)** — a community built around learning openly, sharing knowledge freely, and helping people grow without gatekeeping.

> **The goal is not perfection.** The goal is to build, learn, break things, fix them, and keep moving with a clearer point of view.
